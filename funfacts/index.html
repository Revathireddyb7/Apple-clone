Fun Facts!!! About Animals

  • While lazing around, lions are very affectionate towards one another, rubbing heads
  • Female lions are the main hunters, while males guard the pride's territory and young
  • Lions are the only cats that live in groups, called prides, which can have up to 30 lions
  • Swans lay the largest eggs of any bird that can fly
  • Swans are devoted partners who choose a mate and stay with them for life
  • Black swans do exist, despite the common belief that they didn't or were very rare
  • Cows has BestFriends
  • Cows have great memories and can recognize faces and remember directions to grazing spots and watering holes
  • Cows have 32 teeth and can chew up to 50 times per minute. They don't have upper front teeth, so they use their sharp bottom teeth to cut grass
  • Jellyfish are made up of about 95% water
  • Jellyfish are simple organisms that have a basic nervous system at the base of their tentacles
  • Jellyfish have survived all five mass extinction events on Earth

  • Mother kangaroos may drop their joeys to flee from predators

  • Butterflies taste with thier feet
  • Butterflies have large eyes made of hundreds of tiny lenses
  • Butterfly wings are covered in tiny scales and are often colorful to attract other butterflies or warn predators
  • Giraffes have no vocal chords
  • Giraffes have a sweet tooth, and mangoes are a favorite treat
  • Giraffes have long tongues that can appear bluish, purple, or almost black
  • Pigs have excellent memories
  • Pigs love belly rubs!
  • Mother pigs sing to their babies
  • Pigs are considered to be one of the cleanest animals in the world.
  • Pigs communicate with each other using a variety of sounds, including squeals and grunts. They can make over 20 different sounds, each with a different meanins
  • Octopuses have three hearts
  • Octopuses have powerful suckers on their arms that they use to stick to things
  • Octopuses have large, complex brains with excellent long-term memory and problem-solving skills
  • Elephants are afraid of mice
  • Elephants can get drunk from the marula tree
  • Elephants evolved from ancestors that didn't have trunks 40 million years ago
  • Elephants can understand pointing without being trained to do so
  • Elephants are vegetarians that eat about 250 kilograms of plants per day. They eat grass, bamboo, vines, perennials, and ripe fruits
  • Sharks are older than trees
  • Most sharks have great night vision because their eyeballs have a reflective layer called a tapetum
  • Sharks have eight senses, including hearing, smell, vision, taste, and three senses that humans don't have: lateral line, pit organs, and Lorenzini
  • Sharks can lose over 30,000 teeth in their lifetime. They have several rows of teeth, and their teeth are coated in fluoride to prevent decay
